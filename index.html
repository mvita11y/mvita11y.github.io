My Priorities: Representation Rooted in Listening

Representation rooted in listening, data, and designing systems that include every Missourian.

Defend Reproductive Rights

I will protect the will of the voters. Healthcare decisions belong to women and doctors, not politicians.

  • Protect and expand access to reproductive healthcare.
  • Expand access to affordable health coverage.

Protect Our Public Schools

We must fully fund our schools and protect our local control. We chose Lindbergh because our child is guaranteed stable, comprehensive support.

  • Block effort to force charters into St. Louis County.
  • Fully fund special education and IEP services.
  • Stop all legislative attempts to divert public funds to private vouchers.

Support Working Families

I will defend paid sick leave and fight for policies that recognize the value and dignity of every worker in our district.

  • Defend the Paid Sick Leave mandate.
  • Support affordable childcare and family tax relief.

Defend Local Control

I will oppose any attempt to silence the clear voice of HD-96 voters.

  • Protect the ballot initiative process.
  • Oppose partisan gerrymandering and voter suppression efforts.

Funding Public Safety

Safety requires investment in enforcement, tough sentences for violent crime, and effective mental health resources.

  • Support law enforcement.
  • Invest in drug treatment and diversion courts.

Infrastructure Investment

I will fight to bring our fair share of state dollars back home for roads, bridges, and essential services.

  • Prioritize repair of aging local infrastructure.
  • Protect our tax dollars from wealthy special interest handouts.

Why I Am Running

Becca and I demand a better Missouri that truly works for and represents us. Our values are the values of this district, and this campaign is about ensuring we have a representative who listens.

My Personal Stake in Public Education

My commitment to supporting public education is deeply personal. We chose the Lindbergh School District because our child, who receives special education services under an IEP, is guaranteed the stable, comprehensive support they need. We know that stability matters for every family.

Principles of Leadership

For over a decade, my professional work has focused on building digital platforms that work for everyone, including people with disabilities.

This work taught me two essential principles of effective leadership that I will bring to Jefferson City:

1. Intentionally Inclusive: We must listen to diverse voices. When you exclude people from decisions that affect them, you build systems that exclude them from participating.

2. Data-Driven, Not Ideological: Good governance is about finding what actually works for real people, respecting both the data and the voices that inform it.

I will bring these principles to Jefferson City to ensure our district is truly heard.
